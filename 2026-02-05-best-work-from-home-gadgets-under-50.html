12 Best WFH Gadgets Under $50

Cheap stuff that doesn't feel cheap. Every item under fifty bucks.

Two grand on a home office is insane. Most of the stuff that actually improved my desk cost less than a sandwich. Here's twelve things under fifty bucks. All tested. Sorted cheap to less cheap.

01

Magnetic Cable Clips (6 Pack)

Your cables snap into place with a click. They stay exactly where you put them. It costs $8 and it will change how your desk feels.

I lost a charger behind my desk three times in one week before I bought these. They're magnetic clips. Stick them to the edge, cables snap in. I've got one for USB-C, one for Lightning, one for my headphones. Eight bucks for six. The magnets are strong enough that even a heavy braided cable stays put. Took ninety seconds to set up. My desk hasn't been messy since.

Best for: Anyone whose cables constantly slide off their desk. ~$8

02

Bamboo Monitor Riser Shelf

Lifts your monitor to eye level and creates storage space underneath. Your neck stops hurting and your desk gets cleaner at the same time.

Your monitor is too low. Flat on the desk, you're looking down all day. Your neck knows it. A bamboo riser adds four or five inches and gives you a shelf underneath for whatever. Keyboard, notebook, random junk. Bamboo holds a 27 inch monitor fine. Twelve bucks. Cable slots in the back. Should've bought one years ago.

Best for: Anyone looking down at their screen all day. ~$12

03

LED Bias Light Strip

$15 LED strip behind your monitor. Reduces eye strain. Makes your desk look like it belongs on YouTube. Best bang for your buck on this entire list.

Stick it behind your monitor. Plug in USB. Set warm white. Done. The glow cuts the contrast between the bright screen and the dark wall. That contrast is what makes your eyes tired after a few hours. Fifteen bucks. Every YouTuber has one. You'll keep it on because honestly it makes your setup look expensive. Check out our full lighting guide for more on office lighting.

Best for: Everyone. Seriously. Just get one. ~$15

04

Aluminum Laptop Stand

Lifts your laptop to eye level. Aluminum matches your MacBook. Your neck and your posture will thank you within the first hour.

Same deal as the riser, but for laptops. Aluminum. Lifts the screen up so you quit hunching. The metal pulls heat away. Fans stay quieter. Most of them fold flat. Get one that adjusts height. Throw a Bluetooth keyboard in front of it and that's basically a desktop for seventy bucks.

Best for: Laptop users without an external monitor. ~$25

05

Keychron Desk Mat

Big, smooth, stitched edges. Makes your keyboard sound better and your desk look intentional. $25 for a completely different vibe.

A desk mat ties everything together. Keyboard and mouse on one clean surface instead of scattered on bare wood. Keychron makes theirs for keyboard nerds, so the surface deadens typing noise. Stitched edges. Rubber bottom. 900mm wide, fits keyboard and mouse with room. Looks more gamer than executive but honestly it just makes the desk look clean. Twenty five bucks. For a deeper dive, see our full desk mat guide.

Best for: Making your desk look intentional for $25. ~$25

06

Yamazaki Tower Desk Organizer

Japanese minimalist steel and wood. Phone slot, pen holder, card tray. Looks like it costs $200. Costs $28. Everyone asks where you got it.

Powder coated steel, wood accent bar, Japanese minimalist. Phone slot, pen holder, little tray for earbuds. Looks like it cost two hundred bucks. It's twenty eight. Drop your phone in the slot when you sit down. Pens in back. Everything has a spot. People will ask where you got it. That's how you know it's good. We covered this in our desk organization guide too.

Best for: Making your desk look expensive on a budget. ~$28

07

Deltahub Carpio 2.0 Wrist Rest

Slides with your mouse instead of sitting still. Your wrist stays supported no matter where your cursor goes. It's a wrist rest that actually works.

Normal wrist rests sit in one spot. Your hand doesn't. The Carpio has a smooth bottom and slides wherever your mouse goes. Wrist stays elevated the whole time. Contoured to the shape of your hand. Sounds minor until your wrist stops hurting after a week. Left or right hand, small or large. Thirty five bucks. If your wrist aches at the end of the day, this is what you need.

Best for: Anyone with mouse-hand wrist pain. ~$35

08

Fellow Carter Everywhere Mug

Twist lock lid with a ceramic interior. Your coffee tastes like coffee, not metal. The click when you seal it is unreasonably satisfying.

Travel mugs make coffee taste like metal. The Carter has a ceramic interior so it tastes like actual coffee. Twist lock lid. There's a click when you seal it that's weirdly satisfying. Splash guard works well enough to toss in a bag. Silicone base so it doesn't slide or scratch. Keeps coffee hot for hours. It's a mug. Shouldn't make you this happy. But it does.

Best for: Making your morning coffee feel like a ritual. ~$35

09

Satechi Eco Leather Desk Mat

Vegan leather in Apple-matching colors. Dual sided. Water resistant. The premium desk mat for people who don't want to spend premium prices.

Vegan leather in Apple colors. Space grey and rose gold that actually match a MacBook. Dual sided. Spill coffee on it, wipe it off. Thirty five bucks. Won't patina like real leather but it won't stain weird either. If you want the put-together look without dropping over a hundred on leather, this is the move.

Best for: Mac users who want everything to match. ~$35

10

Anker MagSafe Charging Stand

Your phone snaps onto it with a magnetic click and charges while displaying the time. Doubles as a desk clock. One less cable to deal with.

Phone snaps on magnetically. Charges upright. With StandBy mode it shows the time so it doubles as a desk clock. No more plugging and unplugging ten times a day. The magnetic snap feels good. Angle is right for glancing at notifications without picking it up. Twenty to forty bucks depending on model.

Best for: iPhone users who want a cleaner desk. ~$30

11

Foreverspin Titanium Top

Machined from a single piece of titanium. Spins for minutes. Silent. It's the thinking fidget for people who don't do fidget spinners.

Machined from one piece of titanium. Flick it and it spins for three to five minutes. Silent. You watch it while you think. Not a fidget spinner. More like something from a Nolan film. People pick it up off your desk and go "what is this" and then spin it immediately. Every single time. Forty five bucks. At the top of the budget but it earns its spot.

Best for: The thinker who needs something in their hands. ~$45

12

Koss Porta Pro Headphones

Retro design from the 80s that sounds better than headphones five times the price. A cult classic for a reason. $35 and they fold up flat.

Been around since 1984. Still sound better than most headphones under 150 bucks. Warm bass, smooth treble. Weigh almost nothing. Fold flat, fit in a pocket. The design is either hideous or iconic, nobody agrees. But everybody agrees on the sound. Audiophiles swear by them. Reddit has entire communities around these. Thirty five dollars. Less than one replacement AirPod. For the full noise cancelling experience, see our headphones guide.

Best for: Music lovers on a budget who care about sound quality. ~$35

The Bottom Line

Everything on this list adds up to about 350 bucks. That's less than one Herman Miller armrest. Start with the cable clips and the LED strip. Twenty three dollars total. Your desk feels different by tomorrow. Then grab whatever sounds good. The mug. The mat. The titanium top that makes you feel like you're in Inception. None of this stuff is life changing alone. But all of it together turns your desk into somewhere you actually want to sit.
